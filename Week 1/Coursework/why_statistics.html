Statistics in Your Field

Now let’s investigate how statistics is used in your discipline.

Find a peer-reviewed article from a journal in your field that performs a statistical analysis somewhere in the paper. You do not need to be familiar with the analysis they do. That being said, if you want to find a paper that uses a statistical method we’ll cover in this class, look for a paper that uses “regression” or “ANOVA.”

🗞 Group Homework Questions

Throughout the week, your group will be asked to discuss the following questions:

  • What is Statistics?

  • What is the role of Statistics in the progression of science?

  • How is dichotomous thinking used in Statistics? What are the benefits and drawbacks?

  • What does it mean for an analysis to be “objective”? Do you believe statistical analyses can be objective? Why or why not?

  • What are some synonyms of “significant” that come to your mind?

  • What are two issues that you see with using the term “significant” to denote “small” p-values?


The reporter in your team will be expected to type up your team’s responses to these questions. The editor in your team is responsible for submitting the completed questions to the Canvas assignment portal.
